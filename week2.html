ISMG 2050 • Week 2 Course Map

Working with Formulas and Functions

Week 2 focuses on building, auditing, and applying formulas and a broad range of Excel functions to realistic business scenarios, strengthening students’ ability to translate business rules into precise spreadsheet logic.

Content Focus

Weekly Topics

Formulas & Functions
  • Build and edit basic formulas
  • Set mathematical order of operations in a formula
  • Use absolute, mixed, relative, and 3D references
  • Use formula auditing tools in a worksheet
  • Work with Statistical and Date & Time functions
  • Use functions from Financial, Logical, and Lookup & Reference categories
  • Work with Text functions
  • Build functions from the Math & Trig category

Application

Assignments

Realistic Scenarios
Guided Project – Courtyard Medical Plaza (CMP)
Pharmacy inventory & insurance/payments
  • Complete an inventory worksheet for the pharmacy
  • Enter insurance and payment data for the optometry group
  • Apply formulas and functions to calculate needed metrics
  • Use appropriate references to keep formulas accurate as data changes
Independent Project – Central Sierra Insurance
Bonus percentages & branch totals
  • Set bonus percentages based on commissions earned
  • Calculate totals by agent and by branch office
  • Use logical and lookup functions to identify business policies
  • Display agent names with their locations
Advanced Project – Mary’s Equipment Rentals
Imported data → statistics
  • Transform imported Access data for better use in Excel
  • Build formulas to compute key business statistics
  • Apply Statistical, Date & Time, and Math & Trig functions
  • Audit formulas to ensure reliability
Challenge Project – Mary’s Equipment Rentals (Extended)
Deeper analytics with functions
  • Further refine and structure the rental dataset
  • Layer additional Financial, Logical, and Lookup & Reference functions
  • Design multi-step formulas that combine several function types
  • Prepare results for management review
Student Differentiators

Competencies

What Students Can Do
Competency 1

Design, edit, and troubleshoot multi-step formulas that correctly apply order of operations and mixed reference types (relative, absolute, mixed, and 3D) across one or more worksheets.

Competency 2

Apply a broad range of Excel functions—Statistical, Date & Time, Financial, Logical, Lookup & Reference, Text, and Math & Trig— to solve realistic business problems in healthcare, insurance, and equipment rental contexts.

Week 2 builds from formulas and references → into rich function usage → culminating in business-grade analytical workbooks.
