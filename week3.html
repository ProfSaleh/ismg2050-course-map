ISMG 2050 • Week 3 Course Map

Creating and Editing Charts

Week 3 focuses on designing, formatting, and enhancing Excel charts and sparklines so students can turn raw numbers into clear, compelling visual stories for business stakeholders.

Content Focus

Weekly Topics

Charts & Visuals
  • Create Excel chart objects and chart sheets
  • Design charts by changing layout, style, colors, and type
  • Manage chart elements: titles, data labels, gridlines, trendlines
  • Format chart elements with shape styles, fill, outlines, and effects
  • Enhance charts with icons, shapes, WordArt, and Alt Text
  • Build pie charts, line and area charts, and combo charts
  • Create specialty charts (e.g., waterfall, bubble, etc.)
  • Insert and format sparklines in a worksheet

Realistic Scenarios
Guided Project – Animal Life, NFP
Expense monitoring & cash flow

Student Differentiators

Competencies

What Students Can Do
Competency 1

Design, format, and manage a variety of Excel visuals—including column, line, area, pie, combo, specialty charts, and sparklines—using chart elements, styles, and effects that meet professional presentation standards.

Competency 2

Select and configure appropriate chart types to communicate business insights (such as expenses, profit margins, cash flow, and break-even points) clearly and accurately for non-technical stakeholders.

Week 3 moves from “numbers in tables” to “stories in visuals,” connecting chart choices directly to the business questions being asked.
